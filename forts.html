Heritage Sites: Maharashtra

Forts of Maharashtra

The Sentinels of the Sahyadris

The Legacy of Stone

From the sea-forts of Konkan to the hilltops of the Sahyadri range, Maharashtra's forts represent a glorious chapter in Indian history, showcasing incredible engineering and strategic military architecture.

Hill Fort

Raigad Fort

Known as the 'Gibraltar of the East,' Raigad was the capital of the Maratha Empire. It stands at an altitude of 820 meters.

Hill Fort

Pratapgad Fort

Famous for the historic encounter between Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan, this fort offers a panoramic view of the Konkan coast.

Sea Fort

Murud-Janjira

An unconquerable sea fort located on an island off the coastal village of Murud, known for its high walls and massive cannons.

Hill Fort

Sinhagad Fort

Meaning 'The Lion's Fort,' it is famous for the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670. It is a popular trekking destination near Pune.
